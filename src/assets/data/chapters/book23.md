The yellow eyes continued to stare, though Alice was nowhere to be seen.

"People are always so impatient with endings," said the cat. "They insist upon having one, even when the matter requires no such thing. You see, the difficulty with journeys is that they persuade travellers that something must happen to them. Yet a traveller may disappear perfectly well, without the slightest inconvenience to the world.
A visitor may stir up a great deal of commotion. Yet when the matter is finished, not the smallest trace remains. This is why good stories, like good kingdoms, continue whether or not anyone remains inside them."

The cat withdrew into the leaves and was soon far away. It left behind a dead mouse, the story, and you, dear reader. It is time to go.
