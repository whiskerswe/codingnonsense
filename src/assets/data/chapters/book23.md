Yellow eyes stared expectantly, regarding nothing in particular.  
Alice was nowhere to be seen.

"People are always so impatient with endings," said the cat.

"They insist upon having one, even when the matter requires no such thing.

You see, the difficulty with journeys is that they persuade travellers that something must happen to them.

Yet a traveller may disappear perfectly well, without the slightest inconvenience to the world.

A visitor may stir up a great deal of commotion.  
Yet when the matter is finished, not the smallest trace remains.

This is why good stories, like good kingdoms, continue whether or not anyone remains inside them."

The agile hunter stared down at the place where Alice had been.

The cat vanished, but the story remained.
