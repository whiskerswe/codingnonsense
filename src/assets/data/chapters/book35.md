The Lobster was waiting by the table, where tea had already been laid. There was some uncertainty as to whether it ought to be served.
“Does it take tea?” someone asked.
No one present had ever observed a Lobster doing so. In the end, it was agreed that tea would not be offered, on the grounds that hot liquids do not tend to agree with creatures of the sea. A small piece of cake was provided instead. The Lobster accepted it with some dignity.
“It is rather dry,” it thought. “But still quite sufficient.”
Thus the arrangement was considered satisfactory by all concerned.
