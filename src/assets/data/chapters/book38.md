A little pattering of feet in the distance. It was the Mad Hatter returning, fully dressed, but without shoes: he came trotting along in a
great hurry, muttering to himself as he came, “Oh! the Mouse, the
Mouse! Oh! won’t it be savage if I’ve kept it waiting!” He appeared
so desperate that Alice felt obliged to assist him, in spite of her misgivings; so, when the
little man came near her, she began, in a low, timid voice, “If you please,
sir—” The Hatter started violently, dropped the tea cup and
the cake, and skurried away into the darkness as hard as he could go.
