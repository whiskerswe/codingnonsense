The Hatter stood as though he had mislaid something of importance.
He removed his shoes and began to search.
“This will do no harm,” he said.
Alice could not see how.
“It is important,” he continued, “to know where one stands—on a spoon, no less.”
The little man took a sip of tea, and went on musing.
“It must be struck before it strikes the when!
The spoon will strike, but without it a hammer must do.”
Alice did not wish to learn more, and turned away.
