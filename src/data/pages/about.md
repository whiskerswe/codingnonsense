Alice is a web project that began as an opportunity to explore Terraform,
which people around me had spoken highly of. It has proved easy to work
with and helpful in terms of logging.   


There was no clear idea of what to build at the outset, beyond wanting to use
Terraform. A React application became the starting point, as it provided a flexible 
foundation during the exploratory phase. Once I decided to base the chapters on John Tenniel’s illustrations for
*Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland* (1865) by Lewis Carroll,
the direction became clearer. The images are in the public domain and have shaped the project throughout.


Alice is also an experiment in using AI as part of the development process, 
without removing the human element from the result. It has been used for 
first drafts of the code and text, while the design and overall direction 
remain my own. Reviewing and refining are easier than starting
from a blank page.  


Chapters are written so that they can appear in any order, and the code follows a 
functional approach and is structured to allow small,
frequent changes. Work proceeds in short iterations, focusing on what is most
useful to improve on a given day. This approach keeps the project engaging
and manageable.  


This is a work in progress. The goal is to offer a few minutes of quiet distraction. 
If you have suggestions for how the experience could be improved, feel free to reach out.
