“Ouch—my feet!”  

Alice could not tell whether the sound was an exclamation, or merely a disturbance among the leaves. She looked toward it, and was met by a large beak and two very particular eyes.
A flustered hedgehog scurried away.  

“I am too large to be your pet,” said the bird.  

Alice thought this perfectly reasonable, and remarked to herself that 
the bird seemed unusually sensible, considering.  

“We cannot be friends either,” the bird went on. “Pray set me down.”  

Alice did so, and wondered what the pink creature could mean.  

“All flamingos speak English,” it continued, “but we shall never understand one 
another. You are not a bird.”  

Alice felt there was something in this, though she could not say what.
