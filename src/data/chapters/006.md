Alice watched the White Rabbit dart off, his coat flapping as though the wind were an accomplice.

He did not glance back, though his pace suggested he expected her to follow.

She supposed that running after him would answer whatever question trailed behind him.

Nothing about the Rabbit’s hurry explained itself to her.

Alice turned and scanned the horizon, wondering which direction he had vanished toward.
