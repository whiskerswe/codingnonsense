A plump little piglet leapt suddenly into Alice’s arms. She held it carefully, for fear of doing it harm.

It regarded her with a certain expectation, though its squeals conveyed nothing at all.

Thinking that pigs, like people, might benefit from a touch of adornment, she attempted to fasten her red scarf about its neck.

The creature didn’t seem particularly concerned about its position.

Alice relaxed her hold.
