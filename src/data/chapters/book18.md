He poised a thing upon his nose,
A long and writhing eel—
'Eel of Miss Fortune! Mark me well,
’Tis thus the fates I feel!’

I pass, like night, from keel to reef;
I have strange power of speech;
As eels do keel and keels do reel,
Oh hapless wretch that heareth me still:
To him my tale I teach.

The farmer’s boy no longer stayed,
Nor cared for word or tone;
He turned him from that ancient man
And left him there alone.

And there he stands to this very day, in the garden, balancing an eel upon his nose.
