ONCE upon a time there were Three Ladies, who lived together in a house of their own, in a wood.
One of them was a Little, Small, Wee Lady; one was a Middle-sized Lady; and the other was a Great, Huge Nurse.

Alice came upon the house as she was passing. Being somewhat hungry, she thought she might call, for such houses often contained porridge, or cake, or something of a like persuasion. The door stood unlatched, and Alice entered.

Within, she found the Three Ladies.  


The Middle-sized Lady stood stirring a pot, though it gave off no smell that could be called food.
The Wee Lady was crying in the most indelicate manner. It sounded less like a child than like something attempting to be one.
The Great Nurse regarded Alice steadily.  


“Are you planning on sleeping in my bed?” said the Nurse.  

“No,” said Alice, frowning at the unexpected question.  

“Then you are planning on sitting in my chair.”  

Alice shook her head.  


“Then you will not eat our porridge,” said the Middle-sized Lady, without turning. “Off you go.”  

Alice considered this. As the Three Ladies did not appear inclined to hospitality, she thought it best to continue her journey.
