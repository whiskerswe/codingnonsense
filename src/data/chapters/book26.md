A voice within a teapot grew more clear:
“I practice strokes. The current suits me well.
Each stroke I take returns me almost here.
Each strike, I think, would serve me just as well.”

They held the mouse between them at the brim;
The Hatter grasped its paws, the Hare its tail.
Its darkened paws performed a cautious swim,
As though the steam itself were some small gale.

The steam rose up. The steam returned once more.
“Have you the hammer?” asked the Hare, content.
They found the spoon where it had lain before
And smiled, as if the waiting now were spent.
